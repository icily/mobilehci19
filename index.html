Designing Bounded & Nuanced Mobile Technology for Children and Parents

MobileHCI'19 Workshop
Oct 1st @Taipei, Taiwan

In this workshop, we intend to bring scholars and practitioners of MobileHCI conference who is interested in designing technology use for children and their parents. Parents are concerned with technology addiction for their children but generally feel loss of control in the ubiquity of mobile devices. We believe mobile design community can contribute meaningful ideas in this space. The goals of the workshop are:

  1. Identifying the values, concerns, and challenges of children and their parents when they are engaged in mobile devices.
  2. Creating a design space for nuanced and bounded engagement for children and their family that designers can use to generate new mobile technology.

Call for Participation

Goal

Mobile technology usage in family is a constant negotiation between parents and children. Parents are concerned about their children’s wellness but often have rules around usage without a clear guidance. We believe mobile technology design community can contribute meaningful insights in the space. For example, by making the algorithms more tangible and understandable to the layman; enhancing the collaborative use between the parents and the children; Design for self-discipline for children; providing features for collaborative screen time monitoring between children and parents; and etc.

The goals of this workshop are organized around three themes:

  • Consider children’ and parents’ perspectives together and identify the gaps between their needs and current design paradigm.
  • Creating a representation of design space of this gap for designers when they create nuanced and bounded mobile technology for families.
  • Devising appropriate research methods and applying theories to study children/parent interaction with mobile technology.

Structure

Morning
  • Casual sharing of each submitted work (talks, prototype demonstrations...)
  • Group design sessions in groups with pencils and papers, and discussion
  • Group presentation & Feedbacks to each other
Afternoon
  • Go through design insights from the morning & map out challenges and concerns that parents and children have
  • Brainstorm whether a solution of a design considerations might be helpful or not to each challenge

User-Centered Design Sessions

Rethink how positioned projects can be applied for the use of children and their family members, from a child's perspective & from parents’ perspective.

Discussions

What choices require special consideration in designing mobile technologies for children and parents? How research methods and design of mobile technology may contribute to the gap between parents’ anxiety of children’s mobile device usage and children’s nuanced engagement?

Organizers

Ying-Yu Chen is a doctoral candidate and in Department of Human Centered Design & Engineering at the University of Washington. She is interested in child-computer interaction, designing for families and Internet of Things. Her primary research is in understanding how interactive technology might help with family mealtimes with preschoolers.

Yuan-Chi Tseng is an Associate Professor at National Cheng Hua University whose research interests span a number of topics including HCI, psychology, design, co-creation, mobile device interaction, digital public engagement, and bounded rational behavior and experience utility.

Chia-Yu Chen is a user researcher and interaction designer. Their research interests lie in participatory design, children-computer interaction design and evaluation. They build technologies for children and family and research how collaborative gaming supports parents’ involvement in child development training.
